Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship means using technology and the internet in a safe, respectful, and responsible way. It involves protecting your information, treating others kindly online, and understanding that your actions on the internet have lasting effects.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law refers to the rules and laws that guide how people should use technology and the internet. It includes respecting copyright, protecting privacy, and understanding that illegal online actions have real-world consequences.

What is AUP?

An Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is a set of rules that explains how you are allowed to use a school or workplace's computers, internet, and devices.

What is plagiarism?

Plagiarism is when you use someone else's words, ideas, or work and present them as your own without giving proper credit. This can include copying text, paraphrasing too closely, or using images and media without citation.

It is considered dishonest and unethical in schools and workplaces. Plagiarism can lead to serious academic, professional, or even legal consequences.

What is academic dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any action that gives a student an unfair academic advantage or misrepresents their learning.

It includes behaviors like cheating on tests, plagiarism, copying assignments, using unauthorized help, or falsifying work.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

Use AI only if it's allowed, be honest about using it, and make sure the final work is truly your own. AI should help you think and create, not replace your effort.
